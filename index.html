About

Engineering reliable, intelligent, electricity-centered systems.

I study methods that help future power systems operate with more resilience, observability, and flexibility. My interests include power system modeling, simulation, protection, machine learning for grid operation, and tools that make technical concepts easier to understand.

This site is a home for research updates, selected publications, projects, teaching materials, and notes from work in power and energy systems.

Research

Research Areas

01

Power System Modeling and Simulation

Dynamic modeling, distribution system analysis, transmission and distribution co-simulation, and scalable workflows for studying large electric networks.

02

AI for Power and Energy Systems

Data-driven forecasting, physics-informed learning, grid-edge intelligence, and machine learning methods that respect engineering constraints.

03

Reliability, Resilience, and Protection

Fault analysis, adaptive protection, resilience metrics, weather-dependent operation, and planning for systems with high renewable penetration.

04

Engineering Education and Tools

Clear computational notebooks, visualization tools, and teaching resources that make power engineering concepts more accessible.
