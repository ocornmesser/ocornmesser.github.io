About

Modeling the dynamics of modern electric grids.

I am a Ph.D. student in Electrical Engineering at Texas A&M University in the Energy and Power Group, advised by Dr. Thomas Overbye and Dr. Adam B. Birchfield. My research focuses on power system dynamics, oscillations, controls, and model-based perspectives on measurement-based modal analysis.

Before Texas A&M, I earned my M.S. and B.S. in Electrical Engineering from Colorado School of Mines, where I worked on adaptive co-simulation tools for transmission and distribution systems with high DER penetration.

Research

Research Areas

01

Power System Modeling and Simulation

Model linearization, large-scale system dynamics, transmission-level contingency analysis, and simulation workflows for practical grid studies.

02

Inverter-Based Resource Stability

Small-signal and nonlinear analysis of grid-following inverter controls, PLL-grid strength interactions, and system-wide oscillatory phenomena.

03

Adaptive T&D Dynamic Co-Simulation

Methods for adjusting DER, IBR, and distribution-system modeling detail to reduce computational complexity while preserving dynamic behavior.

04

Engineering Education and Tools

Open-source engineering tools, Python instruction, mentoring, and computational examples designed for students, researchers, and industry users.
